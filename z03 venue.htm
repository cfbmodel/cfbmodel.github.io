VFH is the Venue Factor (when) Home, traditionally called Home Field Advantage. VFA is the Venue Factor (when) Away.

The factors are derived from a deconstruction of the Kenneth Massey Computer Power Ratings for games played in 2009 through 2020. Games at neutral sites are excluded. Games involving an FCS team are excluded. Teams with insufficient FBS history are assigned the national averages (colored red).

For example, consider the Illinois home game versus Nebraska in 2019. Per the Kenneth Massey Computer Power Ratings at the end of the 2019 season, and looking backward, Nebraska would have been expected to beat Illinois by 7 points (computed as 6.60) had the game been played at a neutral site. Nebraska won by 4, therefore +2.60 (6.60-4) was applied to the Illinois VFH and -2.60 was applied to the Nebraska VFA.

Overtime games are handled differently. If 1 OT, the actual margin is divided by 3. If 2 OTs, the actual margin is divided by 4. If 3 or more OTs, the actual margin is set to 0.

Many values may seem surprisingly high or low. The reason for that lies in the Massey ratings (or any other rating system you might prefer). For instance, if the VFH seems too low, it can be equally argued that the rating is too high. In other words, it is all relative.
